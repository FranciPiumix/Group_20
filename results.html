What has been found out: main takeaways

Average Pollutant Map

What is the geographical distribution of the annual NO₂ and PM2?

Quantile classification method allows a clear visualisation of the spatial ditribution of the phenomenon. In 2022, NO₂ levels were highest in a corridor stretching from the capital region toward Ostrava, passing through Brno and Olomouc—some of the largest cities in the country. In comparison, the geography of PM2.5 pollution was more concentrated in the eastern part of the country, with lower values observed around the capital.

Yearly Concentration Maps 2022

How did the country performe in 2022 according to the five EU air quality categories?

Based on the interpretation of EU legal limit values, the Czech Republic performed quite well in 2022 for both pollutants. NO₂ levels, in particular, fell mostly within Class 1 and 2. It is worth noting that EU standards are generally less strict than the WHO air quality guideline levels; however, continuous updates are being made in alignment with the European Green Deal objectives.

Annual Average Difference

How much the air quality in 2022 deviates from the recent 5 years average?

As the maps tend to red, the higher the value compared to the 2017–2021 mean, on the contrary blue areas indicate lower values, suggesting an improvement in air quality. No2 shows a general improvement in 2022, with the exception of some areas in the capital region, Ostrava and the south-wester border with Germany. PM2.5 shows a sharper improvement, bigger improvements are displayes in in the easter part of the region and around the main urban areas. This could be related to the 2020-2021 Covid pandemic and reconnected to a global consistent annual reduction trend. Considering nitrogen dioxide is primarily emitted by combustion processes in industry and energy supply and road traffic, the effects of the lockdown might no be that meaningful. Moreover, changes in energy use due to the war in Ukraine might have altered the use of coal or other fuels in certain areas.

Urban Settlements

Time-series statistics per land cover (class 5: settlments).

Urban settlements results from the classification of the land cover layer of 2022 according to the IPCC Land Use Classification.
Without conextualisation, looking at the graphs showing the mean trend values of NO₂ and PM2.5 concentrations in urban areas, might be effortless. Firstly as seen globaly, from 2013 to 2018 we find non-significant changes in fine particulate matter and nitrogen dioxide, which mean values are almost stable over the years, or at least they do not show clear patterns. No2 graphreveals a significant drop in 2019 that can be attributed to the cumulative effect of these air quality policies, as well as a possible reduction in traffic, consistently with observed trends in other European countries. In both graphs the further decrease in 2020 corresponds with the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, when lockdowns and restrictions drastically reduced road traffic and industrial activity, leading to a sharp, temporary reduction in pulliting emissions. The slight rebound in 2021 and 2022 likely reflects the gradual resumption of activities as pandemic restrictions eased.
