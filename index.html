Why study the air quality?

Air pollution is a major global health issue, responsible for millions of premature deaths each year. While national governments play a key role in implementing regulations and policies to address pollution, the problem is urban in nature. In recent years, air quality has gained increased attention and become a key component of governments’ agendas, as cities recognise its critical role in shaping public health, livability, and environmental justice.

Where does air pollution come from?

The main sources include human activities such as industrial emissions, vehicle exhaust, burning of fossil fuels, agricultural activities, and natural events like wildfires and volcanic eruptions. Consequently, environmental variables such as land cover directly influence the pollution.

Which pollutants?

Pollutants with the strongest evidence for public health concern include particulate matter (PM2.5), nitrogen dioxide (NO₂), and carbon monoxide (CO).

Where will we study air pollution?

This analysis focuses on the Czech Republic, a country with diverse landscapes and varying urbanization levels. Understanding air quality in this region helps reveal how pollution patterns relate to land cover and population exposure across different environments.
