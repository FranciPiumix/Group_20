Methods

This section describes the methodology adopted in our GIS project focused on Czech Republic air quality monitoring. It includes data collection, preprocessing and spatial analysis techniques.

1. Project Overview

This GIS laboratory project focused on analyzing air quality in Europe by integrating satellite-based air pollution data, land cover classifications, and population distributions. The goal was to assess the spatial and temporal patterns of pollutants (NO2, PM2.5, PM10) in relation to land use and human exposure, using open-access geospatial datasets.

2. Workflow and Methodology

  1. Data Acquisition: CAMS reanalysis data (NO2, PM2.5, PM10), ESA CCI Land Cover (2022), WorldPop population data (2020).
  2. Preprocessing: Clipping to area of interest, reclassification of land cover types, projection harmonization.
  3. Analysis: Time-series statistics (2013–2022) for pollutant levels by land cover class; population exposure calculation against WHO thresholds.
  4. Visualization: Bivariate maps combining pollution and population data; publication through a WebGIS interface.

3. Tools Used

  • QGIS with GRASS/SAGA Processing Toolbox
  • GeoServer and OpenLayers for WebGIS
  • Copernicus Climate Data Store (CDS)

4. Key Outputs

  • Annual pollutant maps (2013–2022)
  • Land cover correlation analysis
  • Population exposure estimation
  • Bivariate pollution-population maps
  • Interactive WebGIS and project website
