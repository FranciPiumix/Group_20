Methods

How to build the analysis

1. Project Overview

This GIS project analyzed air quality in the Czech Republic using satellite-based data and geospatial techniques. The objective was to explore spatial and temporal trends in NO₂ and PM2.5 concentrations and their correlation with land cover and population distribution. The study aimed to estimate population exposure to air pollution and identify critical zones exceeding WHO health thresholds.

2. Workflow and Methodology

Data Acquisition: We used CAMS reanalysis data for NO₂ and PM2.5 (2013–2022), ESA CCI Land Cover (2022), and WorldPop population data (2020).

Preprocessing: Data were clipped to the Czech Republic, land cover was reclassified, and all layers were harmonized to the same projection.

Analysis: We performed time-series statistics from 2013 to 2022, examined land use correlations, and calculated population exposure based on WHO thresholds.

Visualization: Outputs included bivariate maps, annual average maps, and interactive WebGIS tools.

3. Tools Used

  • QGIS with GRASS and SAGA GIS processing toolboxes
  • GeoServer and OpenLayers for interactive WebGIS
  • Data sources: Copernicus Climate Data Store (CAMS, ESA CCI), WorldPop

4. Key Outputs

  • Annual NO₂ and PM2.5 maps (2013–2022)
  • Land cover correlation analysis with pollution trends
  • Population exposure statistics relative to WHO thresholds
  • Bivariate pollution-population maps
  • Interactive WebGIS for public access

Note: Due to team constraints, the analysis of PM10 was not completed.
